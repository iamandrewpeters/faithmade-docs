# Customizing Your Site's Look

The Customizer is your design control center. Colors, fonts, logo, header, footer — it all lives here with a live preview while you work.

## What You Can Customize

**Colors**
Primary, secondary, accent, and text color. Stick to 2–3 brand colors max for a clean, cohesive look.

**Fonts & Typography**
Control your headings and body text. Pick fonts that feel like your church's personality.

**Logo**
Goes in your header. Use a PNG with a transparent background for the cleanest look on any background color.

**Header**
The top section of every page — your logo, navigation menu, and call-to-action button.

**Footer**
The bottom section — contact info, social links, and copyright.

## How It Works

Every change in the Customizer applies **globally**. Update your primary color once and it changes everywhere across your site.

## The Game Plan

1. Start with your brand colors
2. Upload your real logo
3. Pick fonts that feel like your church
4. Preview on mobile before publishing
5. Keep it simple — the best church websites aren't the flashiest, they're the clearest

:::tip
Not sure what your brand colors are? Pull them from your church logo. Most designers can give you the hex codes, or use a tool like [imagecolorpicker.com](https://imagecolorpicker.com) to sample them directly.
:::
