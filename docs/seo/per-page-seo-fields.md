# Per-Page SEO Fields

Your global SEO settings handle the basics, but sometimes specific pages deserve their own spotlight. Per-page SEO fields let you fine-tune how individual pages show up in search results and social shares.

## Where to Find Them

Open any page, sermon, event, or blog post in the editor. Look for the **SEO** section.

## What You Can Override

**SEO Title**
Custom title for search results. Keep it under 60 characters. Make it specific and compelling.

**Meta Description**
Write 120–160 characters that tell people exactly why they should click. Be specific: "Join us Easter Sunday at 9am and 11am in downtown Nashville" beats "Welcome to our church."

**Social Sharing Image**
Upload a custom 1200×630 image for when this specific page gets shared on social media. Great for sermon series graphics or event artwork.

## When to Use These

Don't stress about filling these in for every single page. Focus on:
- Your homepage
- High-traffic pages
- Promoted events
- Sermon series pages
- Blog posts you're actively sharing on social media

If you leave them blank, your global defaults kick in. No harm done.

:::tip
Write your meta description like you're texting a friend about why they should come to this event. Natural beats corporate every time.
:::
