# Customizing Your App's Wording

Tab names, the hero message, and every label your members read in the app are yours to change. You don't need to be a developer — it's all in your FaithMade dashboard under **Church App → Settings → Wording**.

## Where Wording Lives

Open your FaithMade dashboard and go to **Church App → Settings → Wording**. You'll find:

- **Tab names** — the labels on the bottom navigation bar (Home, Sermons, Groups, etc.)
- **Hero message** — the big greeting that anchors your app's home screen
- **Section labels** — headings and button text throughout the app

Every template shares the same Wording settings. If you switch templates later, your wording carries over.

## The Hero Message

The hero message is the large headline that appears on every home screen template — a short, welcoming phrase like *"Welcome home"* or your church's tagline.

Change it in **Wording → Hero Message**, then save. It will show up the next time you open the preview.

:::tip Keep it short
The hero message is displayed large and centered. One line — five to eight words — works best on every template. Long phrases wrap awkwardly on small phones.
:::

## When Changes Appear

- **Web preview:** Save your changes and refresh the preview tab (or reopen it). Your updated wording appears immediately.
- **Installed apps on members' phones:** The app fetches its configuration live each time it opens. Members don't need to update their app — changes appear on the next launch.

:::note "I changed it and my app still shows the old wording"
If you're testing on a physical phone and changes aren't showing up, close the app fully and reopen it. A fresh launch fetches the latest configuration from your site.
:::

## Terminology Your Members Already Know

FaithMade defaults to common church-software terms (Sermons, Groups, Events). If your church uses different language — Messages instead of Sermons, Community instead of Groups — just update the Wording fields to match. There's no wrong answer; use whatever your congregation already calls things.
